## The Real Cost of a Bad ServiceNow Hire

A failed ServiceNow placement costs **$280,000–$400,000** in total when you account for:

- Time lost to a slow search
- Onboarding investment wasted
- Implementation delays and rework
- Momentum lost across the wider programme
- Recruiting again from scratch

Most companies only count the recruiter fee. That's the smallest part of the problem.

## Why It Happens

### Certifications That Don't Reflect Reality
The most common failure mode. A candidate passes the CSA or CIS exam — possibly through a dump site — and lists six modules on their resume. In practice, they can't configure a basic workflow without assistance.

**Prevention:** Validate through the NowLearning portal. Ask platform-specific technical questions before the interview stage, not during it.

### Depth Misread as Breadth
ServiceNow resumes are written to look broad. The candidate who claims ITSM, HRSD, CMDB, and SecOps experience is almost never genuinely proficient across all four. Real depth is usually 1–2 modules.

**Prevention:** Identify the 1–2 modules that are critical for your environment and screen specifically for those.

### Speed of Process vs Speed of Market
The market moves in days. Most enterprise hiring processes move in weeks. By the time your panel interview is scheduled, your preferred candidate has accepted elsewhere.

**Prevention:** Pre-approve a shortlist before you start. Compress decision-making to the hiring manager level wherever possible.

## The Pre-Hire Checklist

Before making an offer on any ServiceNow hire:

1. **NowLearning verification** — confirm the certification is current and valid
2. **Version currency** — which release are they certified on? Are they current?
3. **Module depth test** — a practical question specific to your environment
4. **Reference from a ServiceNow context** — a platform-specific reference, not just a character reference
5. **Salary alignment** — confirm compensation is at or above market before investing in the process
