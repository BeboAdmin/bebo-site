## ServiceNow's AI Pivot

ServiceNow has moved fast on AI. Now Assist — their generative AI layer — is now embedded across ITSM, HRSD, CSM, and other modules. AI Agents are entering the platform. The company is positioning itself as an AI-first enterprise platform, not just a workflow tool.

The talent market has not kept pace.

## What the Skills Gap Looks Like

Most ServiceNow professionals were certified and trained on the pre-AI platform. Their expertise is real — but it's in configuration, scripting, Flow Designer, and module-specific implementation. They have little or no exposure to:

- **Now Assist configuration and governance**
- **AI Control Tower** — monitoring and controlling AI agent behaviour
- **Prompt engineering in a ServiceNow context**
- **Agentic workflow design** — orchestrating autonomous AI agents within ServiceNow processes

These are genuinely new skills, and certified professionals who haven't actively kept up are behind — often without realising it.

## The Hiring Implication

If you're hiring for a ServiceNow role that touches AI features, a standard certification screen is not sufficient. You need to understand:

1. Whether the candidate has worked on an instance running Now Assist in production
2. Which AI features they've configured (not just used)
3. How they approach AI governance and output validation

Most generalist recruiters won't ask any of these questions.
