For years, Data Governance was the domain of compliance teams and cautious CIOs. Important, but rarely urgent. A line item on the roadmap, perpetually deprioritised in favour of transformation projects with more visible ROI.

That era is over.

In the past 24 months, Data Governance has rocketed from back-office function to board-level imperative. The driver? Artificial intelligence. Every enterprise racing to deploy AI is colliding with the same brutal reality: your AI is only as good as the data it runs on. And most enterprise data is a mess.

## What Is Data Governance, Really?

At its core, Data Governance is the framework that determines how data is collected, stored, classified, accessed, and used across an organisation. Who owns this data? Who can see it? How accurate is it? Can we trust it enough to make decisions with it?

## The AI Accelerant

Historically, governance was compliance-driven — GDPR, HIPAA, SOX. Important, but reactive. AI changed the equation entirely. When a compliance team hits bad data, the result is an audit finding. When an AI model hits it, the result is a business decision built on false premises — at speed, at scale, with no human in the loop.

## The Role Explosion

Data Governance is now one of the fastest-growing disciplines in enterprise technology. In-demand roles include:

- Chief Data Officer (CDO)
- Data Governance Manager / Lead
- Data Steward
- Master Data Management Specialist
- Data Quality Analyst
- Data Privacy & Compliance Officer
- Data Catalog Architect
- Platform Governance Architect

## The Platform Landscape

Key platforms driving demand: Microsoft Purview, Collibra, Alation, Informatica, Databricks, and ServiceNow — where CMDB integrity and access governance are becoming critical disciplines in their own right.

## ServiceNow: An Underrecognised Governance Play

As ServiceNow becomes the system of record for IT, HR, and operations, the governance of that data is a serious enterprise concern. ServiceNow professionals with experience in CMDB governance, data classification, and Now Assist implementation are sitting on a highly transferable, significantly undervalued skillset.

## The Bottom Line

Data Governance is no longer a compliance checkbox. It is the infrastructure that determines whether an organisation can be trusted — by its customers, its regulators, and its own AI systems. The talent to build these capabilities is scarce. The window to hire ahead of the curve is narrowing.
