## The Confusion Is Costing Companies

It happens constantly: a company posts a "ServiceNow Developer" role expecting to fill an HRSD implementation gap, receives a flood of ITSM-heavy candidates, and wonders why nobody fits.

HRSD and ITSM are both ServiceNow. They share an underlying platform. Beyond that, they are different disciplines with different skill profiles, different stakeholder ecosystems, and different candidate pools.

## What ITSM Professionals Do

ITSM (IT Service Management) professionals work in the traditional heartland of ServiceNow:

- Incident, problem, and change management workflows
- SLA configuration and reporting
- Service catalogue and request fulfilment
- CMDB (Configuration Management Database)
- Integration with monitoring tools and IT operations

The ITSM talent pool is the largest in the ServiceNow ecosystem. It's also the most fraudulently represented.

## What HRSD Professionals Do

HRSD (Human Resources Service Delivery) professionals implement ServiceNow in an HR context:

- Employee lifecycle processes — onboarding, offboarding, transfers
- Case management for HR enquiries
- HR service catalogue and self-service portals
- Integration with HRIS systems (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, Oracle HCM)
- Compliance and data privacy considerations specific to HR data

The HRSD talent pool is significantly smaller than ITSM. Genuinely experienced HRSD consultants are scarce, and they know it.

## Why They Don't Overlap As Much As Resumes Suggest

Many ServiceNow candidates list both ITSM and HRSD on their resumes. Real depth in both is rare. Before posting a role, be clear on whether you need:

- An ITSM specialist who has touched HRSD
- A genuine HRSD specialist with a narrow ITSM background
- A platform generalist (rare, expensive, and often a compromise in both areas)

Getting this wrong at the job description stage wastes weeks of search time.
