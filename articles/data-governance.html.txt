It was important, sure — but rarely urgent. Every enterprise racing to deploy AI — whether that's predictive analytics, automation, or large language models — is colliding with the same brutal reality: <strong style="color: var(--white);">your AI is only as good as the data it runs on.</strong> And most enterprise data is a mess.</p> <div class="stat-row"> <div class="stat-card"><div class="number">73%</div><div class="label">of enterprise data goes unused due to quality issues</div></div> <div class="stat-card"><div class="number">$15M</div><div class="label">average annual cost of poor data quality per organisation</div></div> <div class="stat-card"><div class="number">4×</div><div class="label">increase in CDO appointments since 2020</div></div> </div> <h2>What Is Data Governance, Really?</h2> <p>At its core, Data Governance is the framework that determines how data is collected, stored, classified, accessed, and used across an organisation. It answers the questions: Who owns this data? Who can see it? How accurate is it? Where did it come from? And critically — can we trust it enough to make decisions with it?</p> <p>It encompasses policies and standards, data ownership and stewardship, quality controls, compliance and regulatory requirements, access management, and lineage tracking — the ability to trace data from its origin through every transformation it has undergone.</p> <div class="pullquote"> <p>"You can't build intelligent systems on a broken foundation. The AI race has made bad data governance an existential risk, not just a housekeeping problem."</p> </div> <h2>The AI Accelerant</h2> <p>Historically, the urgency around data governance was compliance-driven — GDPR in Europe, HIPAA in healthcare, SOX in financial services. Important, but largely reactive. You governed data because regulators required it.</p> <p>AI changed the equation entirely. Now organisations want to use their data — actively, at scale, to drive competitive advantage. And that's when the cracks appear. Duplicated records. Siloed systems that don't talk to each other. Data classified inconsistently across business units. Fields that mean different things in different regions.</p> <p>When a compliance team hits these problems, the result is an audit finding. When an AI model hits them, the result is a business decision built on false premises — at speed, at scale, with no human in the loop to catch the error.</p> <p>The stakes have never been higher. And organisations know it.</p> <h2>The Role Explosion</h2> <p>The talent market has responded accordingly. Data Governance is now one of the fastest-growing disciplines in enterprise technology, generating a broad spectrum of roles across both technical and strategic functions.</p> <h3>In-Demand Roles Right Now</h3> <div class="role-grid"> <div class="role-item"><div class="role-dot"></div>Chief Data Officer (CDO)</div> <div class="role-item"><div class="role-dot"></div>Data Governance Manager / Lead</div> <div class="role-item"><div class="role-dot"></div>Data Steward</div> <div class="role-item"><div class="role-dot"></div>Master Data Management Specialist</div> <div class="role-item"><div class="role-dot"></div>Data Quality Analyst</div> <div class="role-item"><div class="role-dot"></div>Data Privacy & Compliance Officer</div> <div class="role-item"><div class="role-dot"></div>Data Catalog Architect</div> <div class="role-item"><div class="role-dot"></div>Platform Governance Architect</div> </div> <p>The rarest — and most sought-after — professionals are those who can bridge the technical and the strategic. Someone who understands data architecture, but can also influence a C-suite, build policy frameworks, and drive adoption across a business that has never been asked to think carefully about its data before. That combination is genuinely scarce.</p> <h2>The Platform Landscape</h2> <p>Data Governance doesn't live in a single platform. As ServiceNow becomes the system of record for IT operations, HR, finance, and increasingly customer-facing workflows, the governance of that data becomes a serious enterprise concern.</p> <p>CMDB integrity is perhaps the most critical example. The Configuration Management Database is only valuable if the data within it is accurate, current, and properly classified. A poorly governed CMDB doesn't just create reporting problems — it undermines every downstream process that depends on it, from incident management to Now Assist AI responses.</p> <p>ServiceNow professionals with experience in access role design, data classification, CMDB governance, and Now Assist implementation are sitting on a highly transferable and significantly undervalued skillset. Many of the best candidates come from compliance, data engineering, ERP administration, or platform implementation backgrounds. Casting the net broadly, and assessing for underlying skills rather than exact title match, is increasingly the difference between securing talent and missing the window.</p> <p>For candidates, the message is equally clear: if you have worked anywhere near data quality, access controls, data classification, or platform integrity — your skills are more valuable, and more transferable, than you may realise.</p> <h2>The Bottom Line</h2> <p>Data Governance is no longer a compliance checkbox.
