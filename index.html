Market Intelligence

THE SERVICENOW
TALENT REALITY

The data hiring managers need before they start a search.

85% of Fortune 500 companies run ServiceNow. Only ~116,000 certified professionals exist globally. Effective unemployment sits near 1%. This is not a sourcing problem — it's a structural market failure.

01
"The certifications look real. The person can't log into the platform."

Certification Fraud

Exam dumps are openly sold. Voucher codes are shared. Generalist recruiters keyword-match "CSA" and move on. We validate through NowLearning — not resumes.

02
"Every resume says ITSM, HRSD, CMDB, SecOps and CSM. Nobody does all of that well."

The Resume–Reality Gap

Candidates list 6–8 modules; real depth is usually 1–2. A bad ServiceNow hire costs 2–3× annual salary in delays, rework, and failed go-lives.

03
"We found someone great. Our process took 6 weeks. They accepted another offer by week 3."

Losing Candidates Mid-Process

Top candidates receive 3–5 competing offers simultaneously. Only 30% of companies fill a ServiceNow role within 30 days. Speed is a competitive advantage.

04
"Our entire ServiceNow build is in one person's head. If they leave, we're dead."

Single Point of Failure

Many mid-market companies run enterprise deployments with 1–2 professionals. When that person leaves, a platform crisis and recruitment crisis hit simultaneously.

05
"We budgeted $95K. The market is at $135K. Now what?"

Salary Expectations Shock

Companies that don't pay market rate lose candidates immediately and build a reputation in a tight, word-of-mouth community that is very hard to recover from.

06
"We hired someone certified on Washington. We're on Xanadu. They're lost."

Platform Evolution Outpaces Talent

Two major releases per year. Certifications go stale in 12–18 months. AI/agentic features like Now Assist require skills most certified professionals simply don't have yet.
